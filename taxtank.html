UX Audit

Improving UX for a Tax Service

TaxTank is a cloud-based tax platform that helps businesses manage accounting, tax compliance, and financial data

Viktor Shkekin Author

TaxTank is a useful project that helps people manage their taxes in Australia. It helps users understand a complex topic and solve common tax problems. The project also has several critical UX issues that could reduce the registration conversion rate, as well as a number of usability issues. I focused on 13 issues, although there are many more.

Low Button Text Contrast

The contrast between the button text and its background does not meet WCAG 2.2 requirements. A contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 is required for normal-sized text. Users may struggle to read the button label, making them less confident about the action they are about to take.

Low CTA Border Contrast

Users who navigate with a keyboard may not be able to distinguish the focus state because of the low contrast. As a result, they may lose track of which element is currently focused, making keyboard navigation more difficult.

Critical Issue

Unclear Password Requirements

Users can enter a long, complex password and still be unable to register because the password requirements are not explained. The error message does not indicate what needs to be changed, forcing users to guess. Some users may abandon registration instead of repeatedly trying different passwords.

Unclickable Cards

Make the entire card clickable while preserving clear hover, focus, and active states. This follows a widely adopted UX pattern used in many modern interfaces and improves both usability and accessibility.

Inconvenient Autofill

Autofill only works if users start filling out the form from the last field. This behavior is unexpected and inconsistent with how autofill typically works, making the feature difficult to discover and use.

The field label instructs users to search by email, but the search also supports names. The interface hides this capability, preventing users from taking advantage of a more flexible search.

Inconsistent Error States

Error labels are displayed inconsistently across form fields. In some cases, the field label turns red when validation fails, while in others it does not.

Critical Issue

No Focus State for Buttons

Users who rely on keyboard navigation may be unable to complete the registration process because they cannot determine which button is focused. This makes navigation difficult and creates a significant accessibility barrier.

No Hover, Focus, Active States for CTA

Without hover and active states, users receive little visual feedback that a button is interactive or has been clicked. This reduces confidence, makes the interface feel less responsive, and may lead to repeated clicks or hesitation before completing an action.

Small Touch Target

The clickable area is limited to the text, making buttons difficult to click accurately. Users must position the cursor precisely, increasing the effort required to interact with the interface.

Different Labels for the Same Placeholder

Inconsistent terminology increases cognitive load by forcing users to interpret whether different labels refer to the same input. It also makes the interface feel less cohesive and predictable.

Poor Video Search

Move all videos to a dedicated Video Library page. Organize the content into a searchable, scannable list or grid with filters or categories, allowing users to quickly find and browse videos without excessive scrolling.

Non-Clickable Logo

Keep the logo clickable across all pages and ensure it consistently returns users to the homepage.

Broken Grid

Some rows contain one field, others two or three, making the form inconsistent and harder to scan.

Final Thoughts

Fixing these issues would give users a better experience and help the company attract more users, increasing revenue. Most of these problems are easy to fix and do not take much time, but they require UX knowledge.
